# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students preparing for spelling bees and adults who enjoy word games. Primarily played on mobile devices in short sessions — a few minutes of focused practice at a time. The player is actively engaged: hearing a word, recalling its spelling, and typing it out. Context is self-improvement and competitive enjoyment, not passive entertainment.

## Product Purpose

A single-player spelling practice app that escalates difficulty through word length and obscurity. Players choose British or American spelling rules, hear words pronounced aloud, and type their answers. Two attempts per word; wrong twice and the game ends. Scoring, rank titles, and persistent achievements reward skill and consistency. A lightweight reporting mechanism lets users flag incorrect words or definitions.

Success means players return for "one more round" because the scoring system makes each successive correct answer more valuable, and the difficulty curve keeps them at the edge of their ability.

## Brand Personality

**Competitive, playful, scholarly.**

The app should feel like a well-made board game or a spelling bee stage — serious enough to respect the player's effort, playful enough to make practice enjoyable. Not a generic mobile game. Not an educational worksheet. Confident typography, deliberate spacing, and a restrained but distinctive color palette.

## Anti-references

- Generic mobile word games with gradient-heavy, "safe" blue-and-white palettes
- Educational apps that feel infantilizing (cartoon mascots, excessive decoration)
- SaaS dashboard aesthetics (muted gray, card-heavy, metric-driven)
- The cream/sand/beige warm-neutral body background that is the saturated AI default of 2026

## Design Principles

1. **Challenge without intimidation.** The game should feel demanding but never discouraging. Wrong answers should sting momentarily, not humiliate. The "one more round" pull comes from escalating stakes, not punishment.

2. **Scholar's toolkit.** The interface should feel like a well-crafted instrument — confident typography, deliberate spacing, a restrained palette that signals competence. Not a toy, not a spreadsheet.

3. **Progress is visible.** Every correct answer visibly advances standing (score + rank + tier). Achievements unlock permanently. The player always knows where they are and where they're going.

4. **Tactile confidence.** Mobile-first touch targets with deliberate, satisfying feedback. Every interaction — pronounce, type, submit — should feel responsive and intentional. No lag, no ambiguity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. The green/amber/red feedback system must be distinguishable without color alone (icons, text labels, or patterns). `prefers-reduced-motion` must be respected for all animations. Touch targets minimum 44px. Input font-size minimum 16px to prevent iOS zoom.
